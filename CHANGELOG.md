# Changelog

## v1.0 (April 2026)

Initial public release.

### Architecture decisions
- Three-tier hybrid: Earth primary, Moon resource, orbital rotating habitats habitation
- Mars evaluated as candidate and disqualified (fails 5 of 15 Must-Have gates)
- Gravity threshold set at 1G equivalent (conservative default against untested partial-G physiology)

### Documents
- Architecture Assessment (IBM ITAA structure, 36 pages)
- Destination Assessment Data Paper (research-paper-style, 26 pages)
- Methodology and Evidence Appendix

### Data
- 25 requirements scored across 3 destinations
- 15 Must-Have gates, 7 sensitivity scenarios
- Source registry with NASA standards, peer-reviewed research, commercial operator data, independent analyses
- Primary workbook: SpaceColony_DataAnalysis_Workbook_v0.4.xlsx

### Known evidence gaps (not blocking gate outcomes)
- Partial-gravity multi-generational physiology (no direct data)
- Colony-scale ECLSS (no operational precedent; Phase 2 demonstrator gates Phase 3)
- Rotating habitat operational experience (zero; Phase 2 demonstrator required)
- ELEO radiation environment (sparse direct data; modeling plus ISS extrapolation)
- Mars EDL for crew-scale payloads (no flight heritage; does not affect current outcome)

## Planned for next revisions

- Background and Physics Primer document (deep dives on centrifugal gravity, radiation, ECLSS, ISRU)
- Future Horizon document (asteroid mining, cislunar economy, trade, expansion beyond 25-year baseline)
- Refined source registry with per-score traceability matrix
- Interactive web-based scoring tool

## Pre-v1.0 history

Earlier iterations developed the framework, scoring matrix, and initial evidence base. The v1.0 release consolidates that work with Mars removed from the recommended architecture and the research paper added.
