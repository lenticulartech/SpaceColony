## KEY FINDINGS (reference material for all outputs)

### Core Thesis
The transport layer is approaching readiness but the destination layer is 5-10 years behind. SpaceX dominates heavy lift with a cost trajectory toward sub-$100/kg to LEO. Blue Origin is the only credible second source. But no orbital habitat exists beyond the ISS, no ISRU system operates at useful scale, and no closed-loop ECLSS has been demonstrated at colony scale. The architecture from Phase 1 is sound. The supply chain to build it is forming but has critical gaps.

### Transport Layer (approaching readiness)
- SpaceX Falcon 9: $2,720/kg, 170+ launches/year, TRL 9
- SpaceX Starship: $90M list price for 100-150t, 11 flights (6 successful), TRL 7. Block 2 upper stage failures in 2025. Long-term target: sub-$100/kg.
- Blue Origin New Glenn: 45t to LEO, ~$67M, orbit achieved Jan 2025, booster landing Nov 2025, 7+ stages in production, TRL 7
- Rocket Lab Neutron: 13t, $50M target, in development, TRL 4

### Destination Layer (critical gaps)
- Vast Haven-1: First commercial station, 45m3, 4 crew, 10-30 day visits, launch Q1 2027, TRL 6. Open-loop ECLSS dependent on Dragon.
- Axiom: ISS-attached module strategy, first module 2027, $140M NASA contract, TRL 5
- Orbital Reef: Blue Origin + Sierra Space, 830m3, 10 crew, $100B projected, TRL 4
- Starlab: Voyager + Airbus, 400m3, launch 2029 on Starship, TRL 4
- ISS: Only operational habitat, retirement ~2030

### Cislunar Transport (high gap)
- SpaceX HLS: Requires 10-15 orbital refueling flights, uncrewed lunar demo 2027, TRL 5
- Blue Moon MK1: Robotic, 3t payload, first flight early 2026, TRL 6
- Intuitive Machines: Only commercial lunar landing (Feb 2024, tipped over), TRL 7 but small payload

### ISRU (critical gap)
- MOXIE: Demonstrated O2 from CO2 on Mars at bench scale (6-10 grams/hour), TRL 6
- Lunar water extraction: Not demonstrated, various concepts at TRL 2-4
- This is the make-or-break technology for the Moon-as-resource-layer architecture

### ECLSS (high gap)
- Collins Aerospace: ISS heritage, Artemis pallets, TRL 8. Industry leader.
- No provider has demonstrated closed-loop ECLSS at colony scale. ISS achieves ~90% water recovery but still needs resupply.

### Artemis Timeline (as of April 2026)
- Artemis II: Flew April 1, 2026 (crewed lunar flyby)
- Artemis III: Redesigned Feb 2026, now LEO docking test mid-2027 (NOT lunar landing)
- Artemis IV: First crewed lunar landing, early 2028
- Gateway: Paused March 2026, resources shifted to surface infrastructure
- Base Camp: $20B initiative, 3 phases, 10 years, announced March 2026
- Annual landings planned from 2029+

### Provider Tiers
- Tier 1 (operational, architecture-critical): SpaceX, NASA/Artemis, Collins Aerospace
- Tier 2 (demonstrated, scaling): Blue Origin, Intuitive Machines, Vast
- Tier 3 (in development): Axiom, Rocket Lab, Sierra Space
- Tier 4 (early stage): Starlab, ISRU startups, lunar surface construction

### Three Critical Gaps
1. Lunar ISRU (no demonstrated capability, blocks entire resource layer)
2. Colony-scale orbital habitats (nothing beyond ISS, first commercial MVP in 2027)
3. Closed-loop ECLSS (no colony-scale demo, universal bottleneck)

### Gap Severity Summary
- Low gap: Heavy lift transport (SpaceX operational)
- Medium gap: Communications, small orbital habitats
- High gap: Cislunar transport, ECLSS, power
- Critical gap: Lunar surface ops, colony-scale habitats, ISRU
